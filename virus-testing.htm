Virus Testing

VIRUS BANK CHARACTERIZATION

Regulatory compliance testing for adventitious agents in virus seeds of various species to follow 9CFR guidance. These materials may be used in research or final products.

9CFR Virus Testing

These assays evaluate virus seeds for extraneous viruses as specified in USDA-APHIS 9 CFR 113.55. Species tested include: bovine, porcing, canine.

Sterility Testing

VMRD offers Bacteria and Fungi testing methods to satisfy USDA-APHIS 9CFR 113.26 for Animal Origin Products. Assay meets 113.25 requirements.

VIEW AVAILABLE TESTS

Mycoplasma Testing

VMRD offers mycoplasma assays to satisfy various regulations, including: USDA-APHIS 9CFR (9CFR 113.53a and 113.28) European Pharmacopeia (EP), European Medicinal Agency (EMA) (EP 2.6.7, EMA) United States Pharmacopeia (USP <63>)

VIEW AVAILABLE TESTS

Bovine Polyomavirus (BPyV) Testing

VMRD’s validated 21 day assay for the detection of infectious BPyV is based on growth in bovine cells followed by an IFA detection of infective BPyV using a mono-specific antiserum generated against a BPyV subunit protein.This assay can reliably detect as little as 10-7 MOI (0.2 virions) in test articles.

VIEW AVAILABLE TESTS

Senecavirus A (SVA) Testing

VMRD’s 22-day assay for the detection of infectious Senecavirus is based on growth in porcine cells followed by an IFA detection of infective Senecavirus A using a mono-specific antibody. This assay can reliably detect as little as 1 TCID/50 (1 virion particle) in test articles.
